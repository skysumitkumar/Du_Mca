Question:
Make a sentence using the word that five times in in a row.

Answer:
Thanks to Mike Dickheiser for this solution:

Did you know that that "that", that that copier smudged, was supposed to be in italics?

And here is Mike Dickheiser going all out:

Sentence using 6 thats, based on the following sentence fragment:

... THAT that tHaT THAT ...

Did you know that that "that", that that "THAT" sits next to, should be capitalized?

And even 7:

Did you know that that "that", that that "THAT" (that I wrote) sits next to, should be capitalized?

And finally, a somewhat contrived dialog that generates 5 "thats" in which the letters appear in proper order but not necessarily in the word "that":

Friend 1: "Did you hear about Bob putting his bathtub beside the big paper hat with the 'T' on it?"

Friend 2: "No, did he take a bath beside it?"

Friend 1: "Yes!"

Friend 2: "What?! Bob took a bath at that T hat? That 'T' hath not much ink on it and will smudge if he gets it wet!"

here are the "thats" in the last sentence: baTH AT THAT T HAT? THAT 'T' HATh not...
