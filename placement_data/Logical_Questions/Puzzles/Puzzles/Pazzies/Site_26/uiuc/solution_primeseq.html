Solution

Question:
How many primes are in the sequence 101, 10101, 1010101, ...?

Answer:

1 

 Note that the sequence 
 
 101 , 10101, 1010101, ....
 
 can be viewed as 
 
 100**1 +1, 100**2 + 100**1 + 1, 100**3 + 100**2 + 100**1 +1 ....
 
 that is, the k-th term in the sequence is 
 
 100**k + 100**(k-1) + 100**(k-2) + ...+ 100**(1) + 1
 
 = (100)**(k+1) - 1
   ----------------
     11 * 9

 = (10)**(2k+2) - 1
  ----------------
     11 * 9
 
 = ((10)**(k+1) - 1)*((10)**(k+1) +1)
    ---------------------------------
        11*9
 
 thus either 11 and 9 divide the numerator. Either they both divide the
 same factor in the numerator or different factors in the numerator. In any
 case, after dividing, they leave the numerators as a product of two
 integers. Only in the case of k = 1, one of the integers is 1. Thus there
 is
 exactly one prime in the above sequence: 101.
