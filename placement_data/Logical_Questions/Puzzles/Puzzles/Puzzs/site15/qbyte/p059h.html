Hint to puzzle 59: Triangle inequality

Use the rearrangement inequality, stated below, to prove the left side of the inequality.

Let a1  a2  ...  an and b1  b2  ...  bn be real numbers.  For any permutation (c1, c2, ... cn) of (b1, b2, ... bn), we have:

a1b1 + a2b2 + ... + anbn a1c1 + a2c2 + ... + ancn a1bn + a2bn-1 + ... + anb1,

with equality if, and only if, (c1, c2, ... cn) is equal to (b1, b2, ... bn) or (bn, bn-1, ... b1), respectively.

That is, the sum is maximal when the two sequences are sorted in the same way, and minimal when they are sorted oppositely.

The right side of the inequality may be proved using the fact that in any a, b, c triangle, a + b < c.
