Windows Mobile gets a 3G boost
Jul. 21, 2005

Venturi Wireless is delivering a new version of its 3G optimization client software, extending its scope from laptops to mobile broadband gear running a Windows Mobile operating system. VClient for Smartphones and Pocket PCs V.3.1 provides "significant improvements in the speed and reliability of their data applications," according to the company, which claims about a 7X speed edge for all media, whether it's an email, video, music file, or business document.

"Optimization becomes more critical as bandwidth-intensive multimedia content and complex business applications are being increasingly accessed from handheld devices," explains Bassam Khan, vice president of product management at the company. The VClient also "enhances the user experience," Khan says, "by maintaining wireless data connections in poor coverage areas where connection time-outs frequently occur and data sessions are often dropped."

The Vclient connects to a Venturi optimization server in a mobile carrier's network.

The client software can be downloaded from the company's website.
