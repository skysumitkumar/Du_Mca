Placement Office

    The Placement Office is involved in securing placements for students passing out from the institute. The office keeps close liaison with various industrial establishments (both private and public sectors), which conduct campus interviews and select graduate and post-graduate students from all disciplines. The Placement Cell provides the infra-structural facilities to conduct group discussions, tests and interviews.
The Office interacts with about 150 industries in the country, of which nearly 80 companies visit the campus for holding campus interviews. The industries which approach the institute can be broadly categorised as :

  • Hardcore Engineering industries
  • Software Organisations/Information Technology groups
  • Manufacturing Organisations
  • Consultancy Firms
  • Management Set-ups
  • R & D laboratories etc.
    The Office starts campus interviews sometime in the beginning of September every year. Job offers, dates of interviews, selection of candidates etc. are announced through the Placement Office Notice Board. Similar communication is sent to the Notice Boards in each of the twelve hostels.
The office does not compel students to accept any job offers, nor does it recommend any particular student to the industry. The entire procedure being purely voluntary, the Placement Office serves to merely bridge the gap between a job-aspirant and a prospective employer. However, it helps students to plan their careers by providing information about the industries which approach the institute. At the beginning of the academic year, a general-body meeting of final-year students interested in placement activities is called, where they are thoroughly briefed about the modus operandi of placement procedures and the expectations of industries. Advice is given on how students can go about in securing coveted positions in prestigious establishments. Each student is provided with a booklet which gives not only the list of industries coming for campus-interviews, but also a number of other details like career-opportunities, growth potential, terms and conditions etc. which each industry offers.
The Placement Office is assisted by a committee comprising representatives of students from the under-graduate and post-graduate engineering streams and the science streams. The committee evolves a broad policy framework every year. Students members are closely co-opted in implementing these policy decisions. Over the years, based on feedback from the student representatives, a system has been evolved wherein each student is allowed one firm job and one "dream" job.

Training


    The Placement Office also arranges for practical training for the students of the institute, during the summer and winter vacations. The principal objective of these programmes is to expose the students to the industrial environment and enable them to gain valuable experience through hands-on, practical training. Usually, students who have completed their sixth semester are sent for training. The Office maintains a separate list of industries which offer training facilities. Almost all students who aspire to undergo training are given an opportunity.
