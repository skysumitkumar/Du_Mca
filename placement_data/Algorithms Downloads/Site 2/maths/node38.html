Timing the algorithm

Since the algorithm is recursive we expect to get a recurrence relation for the time taken. The following argument is going to be a bit vague in its use of the word `average' in order to keep things simple. As usual we assume that the initial list is a permutation of {1,..., n} and that all permutations are equally likely. Let Tn be the average time taken to quicksort n elements.

The sort consists of:

  • doing the separation; and
  • quicksorting the two sublists.

The above separation algorithm works in time proportional to n, say n. Suppose the list splits into sublists of lengths k and n - 1 - k; this leaves one left over for the separator. Then on average

Tn = n + Tk + Tn - 1 - k
