Chowing Down in the Cays
The Sand family samples the downhome food of the Carolina Cays in this summer Challenger.
June 24, 2002
From the introduction, the Sand family ate on a different one of five Carolina Cays each evening, with the Cays connected in a series of bridges going north from Southern Cay, the sixth island. The 5th bridge cannot be Sunken Galleon Bridge (clue 1), Golden Arch Bridge (6), Royal Reef Bridge (10), or Blue Marlin Bridge (13) and is therefore Sir Francis Drake Bridge. In clue 7, then, the Sands ate on the 5th, or northernmost, cay on Thursday, after crossing the Drake Bridge from the 4th cay where the town of Spanish Harbor is located and Shark Cay, the 3rd island in the string. By clue 4, the family dined in the town of Kingsport on Tuesday evening. By clue 9. they drove through Kingsport only one other evening after having eaten there; that evening must be the Thursday the Sands were on the 5th island. Kingsport then cannot be on the 1st cay north of Southern Cay, because the Sands would have had to pass it to get to where they dined on Wednesday and Friday as well. Similarly, Kingsport cannot be on the 2nd cay, because the family would have had to pass through Kingsport on at least either Wednesday or Friday. So, on Tuesday, the Sands ate at Kingsport on Shark Cay. By clue 9, therefore, they were in Spanish Harbor on Monday. They crossed the Sunken Galleon Bridge onto the 4th cay on Monday (1). By clue 5, the day after they ate at a restaurant in Ocean City, the Sands drove through Ocean City to eat in Queenstown. Since Ocean City isn't on the 1st cay north of Southern Cay (12), it is on the 2nd cay; and Queenstown is on the 5th. By elimination, Delmar Beach is on the 1st cay going north; and the family dined there on Wednesday or Friday. If the Sands had eaten in Delmar Beach on Wednesday, and thus Ocean City on Friday, they would have eaten at Joe's Crab Shack in Kingsport on Tuesday (8). By clues 3 and 14, the dinner at Feeney's Fish & Chips would have been in Delmar Beach. By clue 6, then, the Jamaican Jerk Cafe would be in Ocean City, and the Golden Arch bridge would connect the cays where Ocean City and Delmar Beach are situated. Grand Cay would then be the 1st cay and would be connected to Southern Cay by Blue Marlin Bridge (13). The Royal Reef Bridge would connect the 2nd and 3rd islands. Therefore, McDonald's would be on one of the 4th and 5th cays and Petit Cay would be the other of the two cays (10). But then the McDonald's would have to be on either Blackbeard Cay or Coral Cay, a conflict with clue 2. So, the Sands ate in Delmar Beach on Friday and Ocean City on Wednesday, with Joe's Crab Shack located in Queenstown (8). By clues 3 and 14, Feeney's Fish & Chips is in Ocean City. McDonald's isn't on Blackbeard Cay, Coral Cay (2), or Petit Cay (10). If it were on Grand Cay, then by clue 13, Grand Cay would be the 1st cay in the string and would be connected to Southern Cay by Blue Marlin Bridge. However, the Sands crossed Royal Reef Bridge on the way to the McDonald's (10), so it would have to be the 1st bridge north of Southern Cay. The McDonald's must be on Shark Cay. By clue 6, the Jamaican Jerk Cafe is in Delmar Beach, and the Gold Arch Bridge connects the 1st cay to Southern Cay. By elimination, Mr. Pigg's BBQ is in Spanish Harbor. By clue 13, Grand Cay is the 2nd one and is connected to the 1st cay by Blue Marlin Bridge. Royal Reef Bridge connects Grand and Shark Cays. By clues 10 and 11, Petit Cay is the 4th cay in the string. The Jamaica Jerk Cafe is on Coral Cay and Joe's Crab Shack on Blackbeard Cay (6). In sum, the five cays in order from south to north and where the Sands dined on each are as follows:

    Gold Arch Bridge to
  • Coral Cay, at the Jamaica Jerk Cafe in Delmar Beach on Friday
    Blue Marlin Bridge to
  • Grand Cay, at Feeney's Fish & Chips in Ocean City on Wednesday
    Royal Reef Bridge to
  • Shark Cay, at McDonald's in Kingsport on Tuesday
    Sunken Galleon Bridge to
  • Petit Cay, at Mr. Pigg's BBQ in Spanish Harbor on Monday
    Sir Francis Drake Bridge to
  • Blackbeard Cay, at Joe's Crab Shack in Queenstown on Thursday
    Cays Gateway Bridge to the mainland
