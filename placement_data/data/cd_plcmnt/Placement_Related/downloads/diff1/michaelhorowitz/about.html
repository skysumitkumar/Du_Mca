I wrote my first computer program in 1973. After graduating from college (NYU) in 1975 with minors in both computer science and mathematics, I worked with IBM mainframes for 21 years. From 1975 to 1984 I was a programmer. From 1985 to 1995 I was a database administrator. Nine of those years were spent working with DB2. From 1995 through 1999 I worked in the Research and Development group of a large Wall Street financial company. Since then, I have done both technical writing and editing. Currently I do consulting and web sites. 

The Computer Gripes web site is a hobby, you can read about its origin here. The Java Tester web site grew out of frustration, while taking Java programming classes, in determining the version of Java on different computers. I volunteered to do the web site for the Personal Computer Show when the hosts did not have the time to maintain it. Sometimes I can even be heard sitting in on the show. The MDSG web site is also done pro bono as way of paying back the group for helping someone close to me. I have done other web sites too for assorted clients. My web site related skills include ASP, PHP, Cold Fusion, Java, JSP and JavaScript.

Since 2002, I have been teaching Defensive Computing at Hunter College (Continuing Education). The class was concocted from scratch to help students prepare for and deal with the inevitable computer problems that lead to Computer Gripes. The other classes that I teach all grew out of the Defensive Computing class which is long and needs to be even longer. :-( 

As the license plate below implies, I live in New York City. My neighborhood was profiled in the New York Times and the Village Voice, both in November 2002. I don't live too far from Central Park in Manhattan (picture from Microsoft's TerraServer). Amazon's A9 Search Service has a Yellow Pages feature with pictures (one, two) of my neighborhood.
