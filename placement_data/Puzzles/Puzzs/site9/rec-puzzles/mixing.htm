Solution to the /logic/mixing problem

Mixing Liquids

The two cups end up with the same volume of liquid they started with. The same amount of tea was moved to the coffee cup as coffee to the teacup. Therefore each cup contains the same amount of its original contents.

[ I get a lot of e-mail about this one from people who don't believe it. However, this is the right answer. If you disagree, please think about it some more, and maybe even work out some examples by pen and paper -- Arlet]
