Solution

Question:
You probably solved the previous puzzle using trial and error, now can you generalize the solution for n straight lines? What is the solution for 21 straight lines?

Answer:
By giving the number as small as 8, it becomes a trial and error logic puzzle, and by giving a high number for example 21-22 it becomes an intermediate/expert level math puzzle.

By observation, 

no. of straight lines(x)    no. of regions(y)
   1                         2
   2                         4
   3                         7
   4                         11
This is the value when x=1,2,3,4...
If you see the sequence y=f(x) has second difference constant
first difference 4-2=2, 7-4=3 11-7=4
second difference = 3-2=1, 4-3=1

Since second difference is constant y is a second order equation in x.
let y= ax^2 + bx + c
Solving this at x=1,y=2 , x=2,y=4, and x=3,y=7
you get 3 equations in a,b,c which are very easily solvable

Finally y =0.5 x^2 + 0.5 x + 1
When x=4, y=11
when x=8, y=37
when x=21, y=232.
