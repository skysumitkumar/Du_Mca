Question:
There are n couples attending a party. Each one shakes hands with the persons he doesn't know. (Assuming each person knows his/her partner) Mary and John are a couple. John asked the rest of the party-attenders how many times he has shaken hands. Each one gives a unique answer. How many times does Mary shake hands?

Mary shook hands with 0 people.
Here the only assumption I am making is that when John asked the rest of the party how many people had they shaken hands with, Mary was not included.
Thus, John asked 2n-2 people, and since each gave a unique answer, it ranged from 1 to 2n-2.
Now John has shaken hands with everyone. Thus, the person who has shaken hands with only 1 person has shaken hands with only John. Thus, the maximum number of hands that a person from the remainder of the group could have shaken hands with is 2n-3 (i.e excluding himself, his spouse and the person who said 1). But this person (call him Y). But this person has shaken hands with everyone else.
Thus the person who says 2 (say, X) has shaken hands with John and Y and no-one else. Now the maximum number of hands that a person in the remaining group could have shaken is 2n-4 since now no-one else could have shaken hands with the person who said 1 and now X.
Continuing this reasoning further, we realise that it is only possible for everyone to give a unique answer if no-one has shaken hands with Mary. Thus, Mary has shaken hands with 0 people.

mia you are right but you are making one more assumption: that John did shake hands with EVERYBODY. I ll try to explain why this assumption is important.

For every person in the room there are 2n-2 possible uknowns (people that person doesn't know) maximum. So the number of unknowns for each person can range from 0 to 2n-2 (that is 2n-1 possible numbers!).
Assume we will be giving a "label" to everybody onto which we write the number of people he doesn't know. As mentioned, there are 2n-1 "labels" (with the numbers 0 to 2n-2 on them).

Now except for John and Mary there are 2n-2 people in the room. Therefore if we give a label to each of these people then we use 2n-2 out of the 2n-1 labels and there is one left (the one for Mary). If we make no further assumptions the last label can have ANY number on it! (which means Mary can shake hands any number of times!)

BUT if we assume that John shook hands with EVERYBODY, then that means that nobody from the rest of the people (the 2n-2 people apart form John and Mary) new John. Which means that none of them can "hold" the "label" with 0 on it (because that would mean he had 0 unknowns and therefore he would have to know John).
Therefore the remaining label that would be given to Mary has to have the number 0 on it, which means Mary has 0 unknowns, knows everybody and doesn't shake hands with anyone!
