Brain Teasers

  1. Triangles (Intermediate)

    Using six matchsticks of equal length, construct at least 4 equilateral triangles in two dimensions. Note - you cannot break the matchsticks! Also, you can assume that the matchsticks are thin enough, so that if they overlap, they are still in "two dimensions". Solutions with more than 4 triangles will be given extra credit!

    Solved by: Mrtheduke (6 triangles), Fred Dour (4), Kenny Compton (6)
    3D solution by: Jugraj (before we modified the question)

  2. Birthday Candles (Expert)

    Your daughter just turned 10, and she wants her 10 candles arranged in her birthday cake in 5 rows of 4 each. She won't have it any other way! Is this possible? If so, how? (Some people suggested breaking the candles in half. This is not allowed!)

    Solved by: Karen Hoerauf, Phil and Valerie & The Roberts

  3. To Switch or Not to Switch... (Master)

    I have three envelopes. Two of them are empty and one of them has a $100 bill. These are pretty good envelopes and you can't cheat by seeing through them. :) You then pick one envelope. I peek into the other two envelopes. Obviously at least one of these envelopes in empty. I throw away one of these empty envelopes (if only one was empty, I throw that one away, if both are empty I pick one at random and then throw it away). Now I ask you to rethink your choice, giving you the option to choose the other envelope instead. Should you stick to your choice, or should you switch your choice to the other envelope? Or does it not make any difference? Remember, you really want the $100 bill, so you are trying to maximize your chances!

    Solved by: Andrea Wiggins, C Bonnell & Dan Higgins

  4. Beatles On a Bridge (Intermediate)

    The Beatles have to be at a concert across the river in 17 minutes. The problem is that the bridge across the river is very weak and is strong enough to hold only two people at a time (only two people can cross at a time). They have only one flashlight and anybody crossing the river must have the flashlight at all times. John can cross the bridge in 1 minute, Paul needs 2 minutes, George needs 5 minutes and Ringo needs 10. can they make it to the concert in time? If so, how?

    (This was apparently a Microsoft interview question.)

    Solved by: David Bennett, Jugraj & Abhi

  5. Heaven's Gate (Intermediate)

    You die and find yourself confronted by two guards at the gates of heaven and hell. You know that one of the guards always tells the truth, and the other always lies. You don't know which is which, and by asking one, and only one, question you need to figure out which one is the gate to Heaven (or hell, if you're adventurous). What will you ask? (Note: You can't combine two questions by using an 'and'...of course)

    Solved by: Chilly Willy, S. Jameson & Kenny Compton

  6. One Hundred Switches (Master)

    There are 100 switches numbered 1 to 100. There are 100 people numbered 1 to 100 Person x comes and toggles all switches that are multiples of x i.e., person 1 toggles 1,2,3,..., then 2 toggles 2,4,6,..., then 3 toggles 3, 6,9,..., etc. and at the end, person 100 toggles the 100th switch All switches are initially off. How many switches are ON at the end?

    Solved by: Andrea Wiggins, Dan Higgins & Kathy Dunham

  7. The Fly (Expert)

    There are two cars facing each other and are 10 miles apart (bumper to bumper). There is a fly sitting on one of the car's bumpers. The two cars start moving towards each other at 5 miles/hour. The fly simultaneously starts flying (with an average speed of 15 miles/hour) towards the other car's bumper. When it reaches the other car's bumper, it instantaneously changes direction and starts flying towards the other car's bumper. The fly repeats this and keeps flying from bumper to bumper until the two cars collide, and the fly meets an untimely death. Calculate the total distance that the fly flies.

    Solved by: Keith and Beth Perlin, C Bonnell, Brewhaus and Jim Cooper

  8. Spurious Tablets (Expert)

    In a chemical plant, there are 10 machines that produce tablets. All but 1 machine (the defective one!) produce 10 gram tablets. The defective machine produces 9 gram tablets. You are allowed to take as many tablets from all or any of the machines once and only once. You are allowed only one weighing on an electronic weighing scale (i.e., it will tell you the exact weight in grams). After this weighing you should be able to point out the defective machine. How would you do it? (Note: you have no estimation skills and simply rely on the weighing machine)

    Solved by: Andy K, Keith and Beth Perlin, Guy Huysmans & C Bonnell

  9. Sequence (Intermediate)

    p, n, ?, s, j, m, e, v, m - What's the missing letter in the sequence?

    Solved by: Shayne and Lisa, Joan Beausoleil & Dan Higgins
