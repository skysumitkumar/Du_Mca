Question:
What is the probability that at least two people in the world have the same number of friends? Assume that friendship is mutual. Explain your answer.

Answer:
The probability is 1.

Thanks to John Tripp for this explanation!

Let us assume that is possible for everyone in the world to have a different number of friends.

W = World Population.

Since the maximum number of friendships for anyone is W-1, We can enumerate the population F = 0 to W-1, where F will equal the number of friendships Individual member of the population has.

So for individual 1, n=0 No Friendships

Now the individual with no friendships by definition can not have any friends. But individual W will need w-1 friends. But the available population is only w-2, after removing individual 1(0 Friends) and of course individaul W. So assumption can not be true. Individual W MUST have the same number of friends as someone else.

Of course, the situation becomes critical much sooner that at individual W. But this is sufficient to prove probability of 1.
