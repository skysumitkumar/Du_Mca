Solution

Question:
Four cannibalistic spiders are placed one in each corner of a square room. The length of each side is 10 feet. They are released simultaneously and each spider starts moving directly towards the neighbour on its right at the same constant speed. How far will each spider move before they all meet in the middle of the room? Explain your answer.

Answer:
10 feet

Thanks to Michael Wais for the explanation!

Since each spider is heading towards the one on its right, they will all be traveling perpendicular to each other. As they spiral in towards the center, each spider adjusts its angle to remain headed directly toward the next - hence always traveling at a 90 degree angle from each other. So while each spider is always moving, the movement of any one spider does not increase it's distance from its pursuer. As a result, the total distance traveled for each will be 10 feet - the original distance.
