Solution

Question:
Find the value of this arithmetic expression: sqrt(2 * sqrt(2 * sqrt(2 * sqrt(2 * sqrt(2* ........

This goes on infinitely, followed by infinite close-parentheses. :-)

Answer:
The answer is 2.

There are two ways of looking at this

  1. Let x = sqrt(2 * sqrt(2 * sqrt(2 * sqrt(2 * sqrt(2* ........
    Therefore x = sqrt(2 * x) - Hey, that's the beauty of infinity!
    x^2 = 2x
    x = 2
    Note that 0 is also a solution of x^2 = 2x, but not of x = sqrt(2x) since sqrt(w) implies a positive root.

  2. If you're not satisfied with the argument above, here's a cleaner way:

    sqrt(2 * sqrt(2 * sqrt(2 * sqrt(2 * sqrt(2* ........
    = 2^1/2 * 2^ 1/4 * 2^1/8....
    = 2^(1/2 + 1/4 + 1/8 + 1/16 + ....)
    Now (1/2 + 1/4 + 1/8 + 1/16 + ....) is a progression that converges to 1
    Therefore we get 2^1 = 2
