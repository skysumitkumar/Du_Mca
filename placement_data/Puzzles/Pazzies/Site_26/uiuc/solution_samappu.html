Question:
Sam and Appu are the greatest mathematicians ever born. Both of them have two prime numbers on their foreheads and are told that any three of them form the sides of a triangle with prime perimeter. They take turns stating whether they can deduce the numbers on their own foreheads. Sam sees 13 and 17 on Appu's forehead and each of them has stated "Don't know" on each of their first two turns. It is now Sam's third turn. What are the numbers on Sam's forehead?

Answer:
The answer is 13 and 17. The logic is as follows: Corresponding to 13 and 17 (pair on Appu's head)there are just 3 combinations which satisfy prime perimeter triangle condition:

a) 7,17
b) 11,13
c) 13,17

If Sam had 7 and 17, Appu would have guessed 13 and 17 in his first guess as that is the only possible solution.

If Sam had 11 and 13, Appu had one of the 2 choices to make a)5,13 b)13,17 but Appu being an intelligent mathematician would never had chosen 5,13 coz if that was the case, his counterpart would have guessed 11 and 13 in his first guess. But since Sam passed on his first attempt, Apu knows it cannot be 5 and 13 Incidentally Appu is not able to guess it in the second turn also so even 11, 13 as an option is ruled out.

The last option is 13 and 17 which like before has 3 possibilities. Since both pass first two times only that option remains and Sam guesses it right in his third attempt.
